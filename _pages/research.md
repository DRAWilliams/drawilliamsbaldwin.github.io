# Research Areas

## X-ray Binaries
X-ray binaries host compact objects (black holes or neutron stars) that intermittently accrete material from a companion star. During outbursts, they produce powerful radio jets whose evolution can be tracked over days to months.

My work investigates:

- Jet launching and quenching  
- Correlations between radio and X-ray luminosity  
- Transient relativistic ejecta  
- Long-term variability across multiple outburst cycles  

---

## Low-Luminosity AGN
Most galaxies contain supermassive black holes, but many are under-fed and radiatively inefficient. These **low-luminosity AGN (LLAGN)** can still produce compact radio jets observable with high-resolution instruments.


I study:

- Parsec-scale jet structure  
- Accretion–outflow coupling in low-luminosity systems  
- Environmental interactions and jet collimation  

---

## Radio Transients
The radio sky is rich with explosive astrophysical events — from stellar flares and cataclysmic variables to supernovae and gamma‑ray bursts.

My work aims to:

- Identify new classes of faint radio transients  
- Characterise variability on time scales from minutes to years  
- Connect radio emission to multiwavelength counterparts  

Radio observations provide a unique view of high-energy processes, particle acceleration, and shock interactions.
